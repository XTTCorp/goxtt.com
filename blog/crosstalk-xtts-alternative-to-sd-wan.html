CrossTalk, XTT's alternative to SD-WAN

Mar 13, 2022

The IT industry is full of scramble over the urgent move to SD-WAN. However, for most customers, SD-WAN either doesn't make sense or the cost and other barriers to entry are to great. However, XTT has worked to provide an alternative.

What is CrossTalk?

CrossTalk Circuit Symmetry, better known as just CrossTalk, is an advanced bonded circuit technology provided by XTT Managed Access customers allowing them to create a blazing fast and reliable network without any of the high-throughput servers and routers needed for SD-WAN. CrossTalk uses what is called circuit bonding to tie multiple circuits (whether physical or wireless) into one allowing for not only redundancy but also an increase in speed. By sending data over multiple lines you open up the amount of bandwidth available to you. And if one of those circuits goes down, then you're good! The other circuits will continue to operate and pick up the slack from the out circuit, all while we work behind the schenes to bring the down circuit back up!

Is it for my business?

While CrossTalk and other Bonded Circuits may be a good idea, they're not for everyone. Unlike SD-WAN there is no inherent security benefit to CrossTalk, but then again not everyone needs a large enterprise security plan. While cheaper than most SD-WAN solutions, its still more expensive than Managed Access services. If you don't need increased bandwidth and/or don't suffer from frequent disconnects then you will do just fine with Managed Access with Failover. This service adds an additional 4G/5G router onto our Network Bridge equipment that will switch on anytime your primary internet goes out.

I'm sold! How do I sign up?

Appreciate the enthusiasm! However, not everyone is eligible for CrossTalk Circuit Symmetry. Eligibility is dependent on a number of factors, the biggest of which are the Local Internet Providers in your area. If you live in a city with multiple providers and multiple types of circuits available then it will work great for you! If your city only has one carrier available then there's a chance we can't offer you CrossTalk. However, even in this situation we may be able to create a redundant network with a slightly increased throughput leveraging the CrossTalk technology but with 4G and 5G or other wireless circuits.
